What is your birthday?

January 2019, Birthday Problem

How many people does there need to be in a room before there will be a pair of people with the same birthday? Surprisingly, with just 23 people there is a 50% chance that there will be pair. At 70 people, the probability increases to 99.9%. This principle in probability theory is called the birthday problem. Generate the heatmap below and find your birthday. What will the weather be like tomorrow?

February 2019, Predictive Analytics

Predicting the future is a challenging endeavor. Just ask your local weather man. By using models (what has happened under similar conditions in the past), meteorologists develop predictions about what will happen and assign probabilities to those outcomes. The goal of someone who makes forecasts is not to be perfectly accurate (they will always fall short of a goal like that), but to minimize the variances between their predictions and the actual outcomes. What do you think the weather will be like in the next five days in Wichita Falls, Texas? How does your prediction compare to open weather man?

Do you live in a small town?

February 2019, Descriptive Analytics, Making Comparisons

How can two people describe the same city as "small" and "big"? It's usually a matter of perspective. Maybe they have once lived in a major metropolitan city like New York. Using that as a frame of reference will make any town seem small in comparison. Maybe they grew up in a rural farming community. Using that as a perspective most population centers would seem large. Data analysis and visualization can help add perspective and a better understanding of our world. Input a population range and see where Wichita Falls, Texas sits in relation to cities across the United States.
